Getting Started

  1. 1 Open Song Buddy. Each song you work on lives in its own .songbuddy file — tap New Document to create one, or open an existing one from Files.
  2. 2 You'll see two options: Separate Audio… to split a song into its individual parts (stems), or Add Track… to bring in an audio file as-is.
  3. 3 On Mac, you can also drag and drop an audio file directly onto the window.
  4. 4 Once your stems are ready, the waveforms for each part appear as coloured rows. Tap or click anywhere on a waveform to jump to that point in the song.
💡 Song Buddy supports any audio format your device can read — MP3, AAC, FLAC, WAV, AIFF, and more.
✂️

Separating a Song into Stems

  1. 1 Tap Separate Audio… and pick an audio file from your device.
  2. 2 Song Buddy will separate the song into up to 6 stems: vocals, drums, bass, guitar, piano, and other. The whole process runs on your device — no internet required.
  3. 3 A progress bar shows how far along the separation is. Most songs take a few minutes. You can cancel at any time.
  4. 4 When separation is complete, all stems appear as coloured waveform rows ready to play.
💡 Separation quality depends on the source recording. Clean studio recordings tend to separate more cleanly than live recordings with heavy reverb or distortion.
🎚️

Playback Controls

  1. 1 Tap Play / Pause to start or stop playback. The return-to-start button jumps back to the beginning.
  2. 2 Mute a stem by tapping the speaker icon on its row — the waveform dims to show it's silent.
  3. 3 Solo a stem to hear only that part. All other stems are silenced while a stem is soloed. Tap solo again to release it.
  4. 4 Drag the volume slider on each stem row to balance the mix exactly how you want it.
  5. 5 The master volume control (speaker icon in the toolbar on Mac, or the Master sheet on iOS) adjusts the overall output level.
  6. 6 Tap or click anywhere on a waveform to seek to that position. On Mac, you can also drag the playhead directly.
💡 To practice a bass line, solo the bass stem. To play along on guitar, mute the guitar stem and leave everything else audible.
🔁

Loop Regions

  1. 1 On Mac, hold Shift and drag across any waveform to paint a loop region. The highlighted area will repeat continuously.
  2. 2 On iPhone/iPad, open the Regions sheet and tap + to create a loop at the current playhead position. You can then drag its edges on the waveform to adjust it.
  3. 3 You can save multiple named loops in one document — perfect for marking the verse, chorus, bridge, or a tricky solo.
  4. 4 Tap a loop in the Regions list to activate it. The loop toggle in the transport controls must be enabled for the region to repeat.
  5. 5 Rename a loop by double-clicking its name on Mac, or by tapping and holding the row on iOS.
  6. 6 Delete a loop with the trash icon (Mac) or a swipe-to-delete gesture (iOS).
💡 Set a tight loop around just two or four bars of a difficult passage. Once you can play it cleanly at full speed, widen the loop to include more context.
🐢

Speed & Pitch

  1. 1 The Speed slider lives in the Practice panel — click the tempo button at the bottom of the window on Mac, or tap the gear icon in the bottom toolbar on iPhone/iPad. It sets playback rate from 30% to 100%, and the pitch stays the same regardless of speed.
  2. 2 Start slow — around 50–60% — until you can play the passage cleanly, then gradually raise the speed until you reach 100%.
  3. 3 The Pitch stepper shifts the entire song up or down in semitones (±12 st) without affecting speed. Use this to match a song to your instrument's tuning, or to transpose a key for easier fingering.
  4. 4 Both settings are saved with your .songbuddy file so they are restored next time you open it.
🥁

Metronome

  1. 1 The metronome lives in the Practice panel, alongside speed and pitch. On Mac, click the tempo button at the bottom of the window; on iPhone/iPad, tap the gear icon in the bottom toolbar.
  2. 2 Toggle Enable to turn on the click track. Song Buddy automatically detects the tempo — no manual BPM entry needed. The tempo readout follows your playback speed, so slowing the song down slows the click to match.
  3. 3 By default the beat is detected from the most rhythmic part (usually the drums). To use a different stem, open that stem's menu — right-click its row on Mac, or tap its name on iPhone/iPad — and choose Use for Beat Detection.
  4. 4 If the detected tempo seems doubled or halved, use the ÷2 or ×2 buttons to correct it, or tap re-detect to analyse the beat afresh.
  5. 5 The volume slider controls how loud the click is in the mix.
💡 The metronome follows the drummer's natural timing where beats were detected, then fills in the gaps on the grid — so it stays tight even through tempo variations.
🎸

Tuner

  1. 1 Tap the tuning fork icon in the toolbar (Mac) or toolbar menu (iOS) to open the chromatic tuner.
  2. 2 Play a note on your instrument into the microphone or audio interface. The tuner shows the nearest note name, octave, and how many cents sharp or flat you are.
  3. 3 The bar turns green when you are within ±8 cents of the target pitch — that's in tune.
  4. 4 Close the tuner panel when you're done. It stops listening as soon as it's closed.
💡 If the song is pitched-shifted in Song Buddy, tune your instrument to match the shifted pitch before playing along.
🎤

Live Input

  1. 1 Connect your instrument or microphone. On Mac, open the floating Input panel from the toolbar. On iPhone/iPad, tap the Input option from the toolbar menu — or from the Input button on the opening screen, so you can set up and monitor your sound before opening any song.
  2. 2 Select your input device from the picker at the top. Enable Monitoring to hear yourself through the FX chain in real time.
  3. 3 The built-in amp section gives you a 3-band tone stack (bass, mid, treble), a compressor, and a choice of cabinet simulations — from a clean 1×12 to a closed-back 4×12.
  4. 4 Add reverb with the Reverb knob to place yourself in a room alongside the track.
  5. 5 Save your favourite settings as a preset using the + button at the bottom of the Input panel. Presets are shared across all your documents.
💡 Use Test with file… in the Input panel to audition your amp and effect settings through a chosen audio file — useful when you don't have your instrument plugged in.
📤

Exporting Stems

  1. 1 On Mac, go to File → Export tracks… (⌘E). A save panel opens — choose a folder and Song Buddy copies all stem files there.
  2. 2 On iPhone/iPad, long-press a stem row and tap Export to share the individual stem file via AirDrop, Files, or any other app.
  3. 3 Stems are exported as Apple Lossless (ALAC) m4a files — lossless quality at roughly half the size of WAV.
  4. 4 You can import exported stems into any DAW (Logic Pro, GarageBand, Ableton, etc.) for further editing or mixing.
⚙️

Settings

  1. 1 On Mac, open Settings via the Song Buddy menu → Settings… (⌘,). On iPhone/iPad, settings are accessible from the toolbar menu inside each document.
  2. 2 Default export folder (Mac) — Choose a folder that Song Buddy will open automatically when you export tracks, so you don't have to navigate every time.
  3. 3 Audio input / output device (Mac) — Choose which interface Song Buddy uses for playback and for the live input chain.
  4. 4 Default master volume — Sets the starting volume for new documents.
  5. 5 Show welcome screen again — Brings back the intro guide shown on first launch.
